# Voice-Cycle Presentation Overview

This is the text companion to `presentation.html`. It summarizes the slide sequence, the insight each slide is meant to communicate, and the interpretation to keep in mind while presenting.

## One-Sentence Story

In this single-speaker, hormone-anchored analysis, the clearest cycle-linked voice pattern appears in surface/source-quality and timbre measures, while vocal-tract geometry and phonological separability show no detectable counterpart at the current measurement sensitivity.

The contribution is not a population claim. It is a grain-aware analysis strategy for asking where a cycle-linked voice signal lives: whole-recording acoustics, phoneme-level connected speech, and representation-level negative controls.

## Main Slide Path

### Slide 1: Where Does A Cycle-Linked Voice Signal Live?

**Purpose:** Establish the research question and the restrained thesis.

**Main insight:** The project is not simply asking whether the voice changes across the cycle. It asks where any cyclic signal localizes in the speech-production system.

**Key message:** In this speaker, the strongest pattern concentrates in surface/source-quality and timbre measures, with no detectable counterpart in vocal-tract geometry or phonological separability.

**Caution:** This is a within-person exploratory pattern, not a general population finding.

### Slide 2: The Analytical Journey

**Purpose:** Show that the project followed a data-analysis path rather than a predetermined conclusion.

**Main insight:** Each result changed the next question:

- Broad question: does my voice vary with my cycle?
- Data capture: voice, hormones, Oura physiology, and cycle context.
- First clue: longitudinal acoustic traces appeared to move near progesterone dynamics.
- Localization: features were grouped by likely mechanism.
- Deeper grain: connected speech was force-aligned and tracked per phoneme.
- Stress test: frozen speech-model d-prime tested whether the signal resembled articulation degradation.

**Key message:** The analysis moved from correlation hunting to mechanism testing.

**Caution:** PMDD motivates testing a central/prosodic pathway, but it does not determine the interpretation.

### Slide 3: Data Foundation

**Purpose:** Make the data scale and constraints clear before presenting results.

**Main insight:** The dataset is dense for one person but limited by phase balance.

**Key numbers:**

- 59 voice days across 9 cycles.
- 29 voice + hormone overlap days.
- 2 phase-balanced cycles.

**Methods summarized:**

- Voice: sustained vowel plus Rainbow Passage / read speech.
- Features: openSMILE eGeMAPS whole-recording acoustic measures.
- Hormones: Inito E3G and PdG, joined by date.
- Controls: Oura temperature, heart rate, and HRV validate phase context.

**Caution:** The main inference rests most heavily on the two cycles with recordings in both phases.

### Slide 4: First Clue

**Purpose:** Explain what made the deeper analysis worth doing.

**Main insight:** A longitudinal voice-feature trace appeared to move near progesterone dynamics.

**Key message:** The trace suggested a possible hormone-linked voice pattern, but it could not answer whether the pattern reflected biology, drift, recording context, or a whole-voice shift.

**Why it matters:** This is the pivot point. The question became: which part of speech production is moving?

**Caution:** This slide is a motivating clue, not proof.

### Slide 5: Wide Scan

**Purpose:** Move from a single trace to a systematic scan of acoustic features.

**Main insight:** The largest luteal-vs-follicular shifts clustered in perturbation, spectral balance, harmonicity, and MFCC timbre.

**Key message:** Mean F0 was comparatively stable, so the central story is not simply "pitch changes with cycle."

**Interpretation:** The scan motivated mechanism grouping: surface/source-quality features versus vocal-tract geometry versus prosodic control.

**Caution:** The wide scan is exploratory. The stronger evidence comes from convergence across follow-up checks.

### Slide 6: Localization

**Purpose:** Present the central result.

**Main insight:** Surface/source-quality and timbre measures move, while vocal-tract geometry does not detectably move.

**Key evidence:**

- The moved and unmoved feature sets separate in both speaking tasks.
- Prosody dissociation: p = 0.008.
- Sustained vowel dissociation: p = 0.049.
- Formant geometry stays near the negligible band.

**Interpretation:** The pattern is consistent with a signal concentrated in vocal-fold cover/source quality and timbre rather than a reshaping of the vocal tract.

**Caution:** State the geometry result as "no detectable change at this sensitivity," not as proof of no change.

### Slide 7: Hormones And Drift

**Purpose:** Explain which hormone association looked more plausible, and why drift control matters.

**Main insight:** After controlling for calendar-date drift, surface/quality measures coupled more with PdG than E3G.

**Key message:** A tempting raw pitch-estrogen correlation weakened after removing shared month-over-month drift.

**Interpretation:** Progesterone/PdG is the more plausible hormone association for the surface-quality pattern in this dataset.

**PMDD context:** Progesterone's neurosteroid pathway makes central/prosodic control worth testing, especially given PMDD.

**Caution:** Without daily symptom logs, this should not be framed as a PMDD biomarker.

### Slide 8: Phoneme-Grain Tracking

**Purpose:** Highlight the original methodological contribution.

**Main insight:** Connected speech allowed the analysis to move below whole-recording averages and ask whether the signal was global or phoneme-structured.

**Method contribution:**

- Force-align connected speech.
- Recompute features per phoneme.
- Track phoneme-level measures longitudinally.
- Use de-meaning and within-recording controls to separate global settings from phoneme-specific changes.

**Key result:** Most of the signal looked global: a recording-wide setting across much of the phonetic inventory.

**Localized residuals:**

- Diphthong H1-H2 survived de-meaning/FDR.
- Nasal MFCC2 survived de-meaning/FDR.

**Caution:** The residuals are mechanistically coherent but still single-subject leads.

### Slide 9: Negative Control

**Purpose:** Test whether the cycle looked like degraded articulation.

**Main insight:** Frozen speech-model d-prime did not show phonological separability collapse across the cycle.

**Key evidence:**

- Composite effect was negligible: Cliff's delta = -0.12.
- 0/8 contrasts survived correction across 3 model backbones.

**Interpretation:** This supports an acoustic-surface/timbre interpretation and argues against an articulation-degradation story.

**Caution:** This is a specificity check, not a standalone discovery.

### Slide 10: Strongest Conclusion, Honest Uncertainty

**Purpose:** Close with calibrated confidence.

**Strongest pattern:**

- Surface/source-quality and timbre move more than geometry.
- The geometry null is supported by a sensitivity-floor check.
- Phoneme analysis mostly reproduces a global setting.

**Moderate evidence:**

- PdG association after date-drift control.
- Diphthong and nasal residuals at the phoneme grain.
- SSL separability null across three backbones.

**Directional only:**

- PMDD / late-luteal central instability.
- Hormone rate-of-change versus hormone level.
- Generalization beyond this single speaker.

**Closing message:** The project contributes a hormone-anchored, grain-aware approach for localizing a cycle-linked voice signal.

## Appendix Slides

### Slide 11: Sensitivity

**Purpose:** Defend the geometry null.

**Main insight:** The formant measures are sensitive enough to detect large movements within speech, yet show only tiny cycle-linked shifts.

**Key numbers:**

- F1 moves about 184 Hz within speech, but about 1.8 Hz across the cycle.
- F2 moves about 210 Hz within speech, but about 5.8 Hz across the cycle.

**Interpretation:** The flat geometry result is not just an insensitive measurement.

### Slide 12: Phoneme Residuals

**Purpose:** Provide detail for the phoneme-grain findings.

**Main insight:** After removing the recording-wide offset, most class effects collapse, but two residuals remain.

**Key results:**

- Diphthongs: extra H1-H2/open-quotient rise after de-meaning and F0 control, q = 0.023.
- Nasals: extra MFCC2 timbre shift, consistent with nasal/upper-airway context, q = 0.049.

**Caution:** These are leads for replication, not established general findings.

### Slide 13: PMDD Context

**Purpose:** Explain why PMDD belongs in the story without overclaiming.

**Main insight:** PMDD is commonly understood as sensitivity to normal hormonal change rather than abnormal hormone levels.

**Why it matters:** That makes the central/prosodic pathway biologically plausible enough to test.

**Caution:** Without daily symptom logs, voice changes cannot be aligned with symptom severity. PMDD should be framed as motivation and directional context, not a diagnostic claim.

### Slide 14: Likely Questions

**Purpose:** Prepare concise answers for discussion.

**Is this generalizable?** Not yet. It is a single-subject exploratory analysis.

**Could recording conditions explain it?** Some timbre measures are vulnerable, which is why the interpretation relies on drift control, within-cycle checks, phoneme de-meaning, and negative controls.

**Why not focus on pitch?** Mean F0 was comparatively stable, and the tempting pitch-estrogen result weakened after date-drift control.

**Why connected speech?** Connected speech gives internal controls. A sustained vowel cannot separate a global setting from phoneme-structured change.

## Core Insights To Emphasize Verbally

1. The key scientific move is localization, not prediction.
2. The strongest result is a dissociation: surface/source quality moves more than vocal-tract geometry.
3. The geometry null is meaningful because the formant measures clearly detect much larger movements elsewhere.
4. Drift control matters: raw longitudinal hormone correlations can mislead.
5. The phoneme-grain analysis is the most creative technical contribution.
6. The SSL d-prime analysis is a specificity check: the cycle does not look like articulation degradation.
7. PMDD is relevant as motivation and biological context, but the current data do not support a PMDD biomarker claim.

## Overall Confidence Frame

Use this wording if asked to summarize the confidence level:

> The strongest evidence is that, in this speaker, the cycle-linked voice pattern localizes more to surface/source-quality and timbre than to vocal-tract geometry. The hormone and phoneme-grain findings are coherent and technically interesting, but they remain single-subject patterns. PMDD and late-luteal central instability are directional context until daily symptom tracking is added.
