Where does a cycle-linked voice signal live?

A concise data story: I started with a broad question, followed hormone-anchored voice patterns, and tested whether the signal localized to vocal-fold surface quality, vocal-tract geometry, or central/prosodic control.

Ivy Hamilton · June 2026 · single speaker · PMDD context

Main thesis

In this speaker, the clearest cyclic pattern concentrates in surface/source-quality and timbre measures, with no detectable counterpart in vocal-tract geometry or phonological separability.

This is a within-person pattern to test, not a population finding.

Hormones and drift

PdG was the more plausible hormone link after drift control

07 / main

Date-partial Spearman on hormone-overlap days. Associations, not causal effects.

Result

Surface/quality measures coupled more with PdG than E3G after controlling for date drift.

A tempting raw pitch-estrogen correlation weakened after removing shared month-over-month drift.

  • This is why drift control matters in longitudinal voice data.
  • PMDD belongs as context: progesterone's neurosteroid pathway makes central/prosodic control worth testing.
  • Without daily symptom logs, I would not frame this as a PMDD biomarker.
Phoneme-grain tracking

Connected speech let me test localization below the recording average

08 / main

Per-phoneme H1-H2 in within-cycle units. Most voiced phones shift in the same direction into luteal weeks.

Method contribution

Force-align connected speech, recompute features per phoneme, and track those features longitudinally.

This is the most original technical step in the project.

Main result

Most of the signal looked global.

It behaved like a recording-wide setting across much of the phonetic inventory.

Residuals

Diphthong H1-H2 and nasal MFCC2 survived de-meaning/FDR.

Mechanistically coherent, but still single-subject leads.

Negative control

The cycle did not look like degraded articulation

09 / main

Frozen SSL d-prime across HuBERT, WavLM, and wav2vec2. The cycle does not resemble dysarthria-style separability collapse.

Specificity check

I adapted a dysarthria-inspired measure of phonological separability in frozen speech-model embeddings.

Composite effect was negligible: Cliff's δ = -0.12.

  • 0/8 contrasts survived correction across 3 model backbones.
  • This supports an acoustic-surface/timbre interpretation.
  • It argues against an articulation-degradation story.

Appendix · PMDD context

Why PMDD belongs in the story, but not as the headline

13 / appendix

PMDD is not usually interpreted as abnormal hormone levels. It is sensitivity to normal hormonal change, especially around the progesterone/allopregnanolone pathway.

  • That makes the central/prosodic pathway biologically plausible enough to test.
  • I do not have daily symptom logs, so I cannot align voice changes to symptom severity.
  • The correct framing is motivation and directional context, not a diagnostic claim.

Central/prosodic instability by sub-phase. Directional, not confirmatory.

Appendix · likely questions

Short answers for discussion

14 / appendix
Is this generalizable?

No, not yet.

It is a single-subject exploratory analysis. The claim is about a within-person pattern and a method for testing it.

Could recording conditions explain it?

Some timbre measures are vulnerable.

That is why the story depends on drift control, within-cycle checks, phoneme de-meaning, and negative controls.

Why not focus on pitch?

Mean F0 was comparatively stable.

A tempting pitch-estrogen result weakened after date-drift control.

Why connected speech?

It gives internal controls.

A sustained vowel cannot separate a global setting from phoneme-structured change.
